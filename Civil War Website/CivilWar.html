    Beginning battle

    Firing on Fort Sumter

    The Civil War began on April 12, 1861 with the Confederacy firing on Fort Sumter in South Carolina. This happened at approximately 4:30AM Eastern Time.

    Lincoln Calls for Voluteers

    Soon after the firing on Fort Sumter Abraham Lincoln called for thousands of voluteers to quell the rebellion. Tens of thousands showed up to volunteer in the North. In the South tens of thousands volunteeredto fight for the Confederacy.
